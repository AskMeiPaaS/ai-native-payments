Cash Payment — Indian Payment Channel Risk & Routing Guide

Overview:
Cash remains a legal tender in India but is increasingly regulated and discouraged for large transactions. The Government of India and RBI have implemented multiple measures to curb high-value cash transactions under the Digital India and demonetization initiatives. Cash transactions above specified thresholds trigger mandatory reporting, TDS deductions, and potential penalties.

Statutory Limits & Regulations (Income Tax Act):
- Section 269ST: No person shall receive ₹2,00,000 or more in cash in a single transaction, in aggregate from a person in a day, or in respect of a single event/occasion. Penalty: 100% of the amount received.
- Section 269SS: Loans/deposits of ₹20,000 or more cannot be accepted in cash. Must use bank transfer/cheque/DD.
- Section 269T: Repayment of loans/deposits of ₹20,000 or more cannot be made in cash.
- Section 40A(3): Business payments above ₹10,000 in cash are disallowed as business expense (not deductible).
- Section 194N (TDS on Cash Withdrawal): TDS at 2% on cash withdrawals exceeding ₹1,00,00,000 (₹1 crore) in a financial year. Rate increases to 5% if ITR not filed for past 3 years and withdrawal exceeds ₹20,00,000.
- Section 13A: Political parties can only receive cash donations up to ₹2,000 per person.

PAN Card Requirements for Cash:
- Bank deposit above ₹50,000: PAN/Form 60 mandatory
- Bank deposit above ₹10,00,000 in a year (savings) or ₹50,00,000 (current): reported to Income Tax via SFT
- Cash payment for goods/services above ₹2,00,000: PAN mandatory
- Foreign exchange purchase above ₹50,000: PAN mandatory
- Property registration: cash component not permitted above ₹20,000

Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Triggers:
- Cash Transaction Report (CTR): Banks must report all cash transactions above ₹10,00,000 (individual) or ₹50,00,000 (business) per month to FIU-IND (Financial Intelligence Unit)
- Suspicious Transaction Report (STR): Any cash transaction that appears unusual regardless of amount
- Cross-border cash: declaration mandatory for cash above ₹25,000 (foreign currency) or equivalent at customs
- Structuring (smurfing): deliberate splitting of cash transactions to avoid reporting thresholds — this itself is a reportable offence

Risk Profile — Cash is the HIGHEST RISK Payment Channel:
MICRO TRANSACTIONS (₹0 – ₹2,000):
- Risk level: LOW
- Acceptable for daily retail purchases, market transactions
- No regulatory reporting required
- Common and legitimate in India's informal economy
- No special documentation needed

SMALL TRANSACTIONS (₹2,001 – ₹10,000):
- Risk level: LOW-MEDIUM
- Acceptable but recommend digital alternatives
- Section 40A(3) threshold: business expense above ₹10,000 in cash not deductible
- PAN not required but track for aggregate monitoring

MEDIUM TRANSACTIONS (₹10,001 – ₹50,000):
- Risk level: MEDIUM-HIGH
- PAN mandatory for bank deposits above ₹50,000
- Recommend NEFT/UPI strongly as alternative
- If cash: verify identity, document source of funds
- HITL review for: unknown parties, irregular frequency

LARGE TRANSACTIONS (₹50,001 – ₹2,00,000):
- Risk level: HIGH
- PAN mandatory for all cash transactions in this range
- SFT reporting if aggregate deposits exceed ₹10L/year
- Strong recommendation to use electronic payment instead
- HITL review mandatory
- Source-of-funds documentation required

PROHIBITED ZONE (above ₹2,00,000):
- Risk level: CRITICAL — ILLEGAL under Section 269ST
- Receipt of ₹2,00,000+ in cash is PROHIBITED
- Penalty: 100% of amount received
- System MUST reject cash payment options above ₹2,00,000
- Recommend RTGS, NEFT, or cheque as mandatory alternatives
- Exception: government receipts, banking companies, post-office savings, cooperative banks as specified by notification

Trusted Partner Framework for Cash:
- Cash transactions should be DISCOURAGED regardless of trust level
- Tier 1 partners: may process cash up to ₹50,000 with automatic approval, HITL above
- Tier 2 partners: HITL review for cash above ₹20,000
- Tier 3 partners (new/unknown): HITL review for cash above ₹10,000
- All partners: cash above ₹2,00,000 is REJECTED by system (legal prohibition)

When Cash is Appropriate:
- Small retail purchases under ₹2,000 in informal economy
- Rural areas with limited digital infrastructure
- Emergency situations when digital channels are down
- Small vendor payments where no digital option exists

When to STRONGLY Discourage Cash:
- Any business transaction above ₹10,000 (tax deductibility issue)
- Any transaction above ₹50,000 (PAN/reporting requirements)
- Loan/deposit transactions above ₹20,000 (Section 269SS/269T)
- Any transaction above ₹2,00,000 (illegal under 269ST)
- When digital alternatives (UPI/NEFT/RTGS) are available
- Cross-border transactions

Fraud & Compliance Patterns for Cash:
- Structuring (smurfing): breaking large amounts into sub-₹50,000 deposits to avoid PAN requirement
- Round-tripping: cash deposited → transferred electronically → withdrawn as cash elsewhere
- Cash couriers: physical movement of large cash amounts across state borders
- Shell company deposits: cash deposited into shell company accounts to layer illicit funds
- Demonetization evasion: using multiple accounts to deposit/exchange currency
Mitigation: aggregate monitoring across accounts, CTR/STR filing, KYC verification, AI-based pattern detection for structuring
