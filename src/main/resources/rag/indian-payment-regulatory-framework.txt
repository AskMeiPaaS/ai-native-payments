Indian Payment Systems Regulatory Framework — RBI & NPCI Compliance Guide

This document provides the regulatory context that the PaSS Orchestrator Agent must consider when processing Indian payment transactions.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
REGULATORY BODIES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Reserve Bank of India (RBI):
- India's central bank, regulator of all payment systems under Payment and Settlement Systems Act, 2007
- Regulates: NEFT, RTGS, cheque clearing, prepaid instruments, cross-border payments
- Key departments: DPSS (Department of Payment and Settlement Systems), DoR (Department of Regulation)

National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI):
- Umbrella organization for operating retail payment systems in India
- Operates: UPI, IMPS, NACH, RuPay, BBPS (Bharat Bill Payment System), FASTag, AePS
- Established by RBI and Indian Banks Association (IBA)

FIU-IND (Financial Intelligence Unit - India):
- Receives and analyzes CTR (Cash Transaction Reports), STR (Suspicious Transaction Reports)
- Reports to Ministry of Finance

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
KEY REGULATIONS FOR PAYMENT TRANSACTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA), 2002:
- KYC mandatory for all bank account holders
- Customer Due Diligence (CDD) for transactions above thresholds
- Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) for politically exposed persons (PEPs)
- Record retention: 5 years after account closure or transaction

2. Payment and Settlement Systems Act, 2007:
- RBI has exclusive authority over payment system regulation
- Authorization required for operating any payment system
- Settlement finality: once settled through RTGS/NEFT, transaction is irrevocable

3. Information Technology Act, 2000 & RBI Cyber Security Framework:
- Two-factor authentication mandatory for all electronic payments
- Data localization: payment data must be stored in India (RBI circular April 2018)
- Incident reporting: payment system operators must report cyber incidents within 6 hours

4. RBI Master Direction on KYC (2016, updated):
- Aadhaar-based e-KYC, Video-KYC, CKYC (Central KYC Registry)
- Periodic KYC update mandatory (every 2 years for high-risk, 10 years for low-risk)
- Simplified KYC (Small Accounts): limited functionality, max balance ₹50,000, max annual credits ₹1,00,000

5. RBI Digital Lending Guidelines (2022):
- All loan disbursements must be to borrower's bank account
- Direct cash disbursement prohibited for regulated entities
- Fee transparency and cooling-off period mandatory

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
TRANSACTION REPORTING THRESHOLDS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Cash Transaction Report (CTR) — Monthly to FIU-IND:
- Individual: all cash transactions above ₹10,00,000 (₹10 lakh) in a month
- Non-individual: all cash transactions above ₹50,00,000 (₹50 lakh) in a month
- Integrally connected transactions that individually are below threshold but aggregate above

Suspicious Transaction Report (STR):
- No threshold — any transaction that gives reasonable suspicion
- Must be filed within 7 days of determination
- Includes: unusual pattern, inconsistent with customer profile, no economic rationale
- Covers all channels: cash, UPI, NEFT, RTGS, cheque

Counterfeit Currency Report (CCR):
- All counterfeit notes detected must be reported to RBI and police
- Monthly reporting to FIU

Statement of Financial Transactions (SFT) — Annual to Income Tax:
- Savings account: aggregate deposits > ₹10,00,000 in a financial year
- Current account: aggregate deposits > ₹50,00,000 in a financial year
- Fixed deposit: aggregate > ₹10,00,000 in a financial year
- Credit card: payment > ₹1,00,000 in cash or ₹10,00,000 aggregate

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
UPI-SPECIFIC REGULATIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

- NPCI circular: P2P limit ₹1,00,000/transaction, P2M verified merchant ₹2,00,000
- Enhanced limits: ₹5,00,000 for tax, IPO, hospital, education (RBI approved)
- UPI AutoPay: mandate limit ₹1,00,000 (enhanced from ₹15,000)
- Dispute resolution: TAT (Turn Around Time) for failed transactions:
  - Beneficiary not credited: auto-reversal within T+5 working days
  - Penalty: ₹100/day to customer if reversal delayed beyond T+5
- UPI fraud liability: zero liability if reported within 3 working days

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
NEFT/RTGS-SPECIFIC REGULATIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

- NEFT: 24x7, batch settlement in half-hourly cycles, no minimum/maximum
- RTGS: 24x7, real-time gross settlement, minimum ₹2,00,000
- Both: zero charges for savings accounts on online/electronic channels (RBI mandate)
- Failed NEFT/RTGS: credit back within 2 hours of batch/settlement failure
- Positive Pay System: mandatory for NEFT/RTGS transactions above ₹50,000 (RBI guideline)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
CHEQUE-SPECIFIC REGULATIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

- CTS (Cheque Truncation System): electronic clearing, T+1 settlement
- Positive Pay: mandatory for cheques ≥ ₹50,000 (since Jan 2021)
- Validity: 3 months from date of issue (revised from 6 months, effective April 2012)
- Section 138 NI Act: dishonour for insufficient funds is criminal offence (up to 2 years imprisonment)
- Stale cheque: cheque not presented within validity period cannot be cleared

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
CASH-SPECIFIC REGULATIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

- Section 269ST: receipt of ₹2,00,000+ in cash is prohibited (100% penalty)
- Section 194N: TDS 2% on cash withdrawal above ₹1 crore/year (5% if ITR not filed, above ₹20L)
- Section 269SS/269T: loan/deposit above ₹20,000 cannot be in cash
- Section 40A(3): business expense above ₹10,000 in cash not deductible
- PAN mandatory: cash transactions above ₹50,000 in single instance
- Demonetization legacy: enhanced surveillance on large cash movements

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
CROSS-CUTTING COMPLIANCE RULES FOR PaSS AGENT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Always validate transaction against channel-specific limits before execution
2. Apply AML screening for all transactions above ₹10,00,000 (any channel)
3. File STR for any transaction with behavioral score < 0.60 or unusual pattern
4. Maintain complete audit trail: timestamp, channel, amount, parties, device, IP, decision, reasoning
5. Data retention: minimum 5 years for all transaction records (PMLA requirement)
6. Consent: explicit customer consent required before mandate creation or modification
7. Grievance redressal: provide escalation path (bank → banking ombudsman → RBI) for all disputed transactions
8. The agent must never reveal internal fraud scores or detection methods to the end user
