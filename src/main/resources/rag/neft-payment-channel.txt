NEFT (National Electronic Funds Transfer) — Indian Payment Channel Risk & Routing Guide

Overview:
NEFT is an electronic fund transfer system maintained by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI). It enables bank-to-bank transfers in half-hourly batches, operating 24x7 since December 2019. NEFT is the most widely used non-real-time payment system in India, suitable for medium-value non-urgent transfers.

Transaction Limits:
- Minimum transfer: ₹1 (no minimum)
- Maximum transfer: No upper limit (RBI removed the cap)
- Per-transaction limit varies by bank (typically ₹10,00,000 to ₹25,00,000 for net banking)
- Some banks impose daily aggregate limits (₹10,00,000 to ₹50,00,000)

Settlement: Batch processing in half-hourly cycles (48 batches per day)
- Typical settlement time: 30 minutes to 2 hours
- Cut-off: None (24x7 since Dec 16, 2019, but batch settlement means non-instant)

Charges (RBI mandated — effective Jan 2020):
- Savings account customers: ZERO charges for online NEFT (RBI directive)
- Current account / offline: Bank may charge up to ₹2.50 (up to ₹10,000), ₹5 (₹10K–₹1L), ₹15 (₹1L–₹2L), ₹25 (above ₹2L) + GST
- Beneficiary bank: No charges on inward NEFT

Required Details:
- Beneficiary name, bank account number, IFSC code, bank branch name
- Remitter must have net banking or visit bank branch

Risk Profile by Transaction Amount:
LOW RISK (₹0 – ₹50,000):
- Standard NEFT, auto-processable
- Minimal verification needed for known beneficiaries
- Trusted partner auto-approve threshold: score ≥ 0.80
- Suitable for: salary credits, vendor payments, utility bills

MEDIUM RISK (₹50,001 – ₹5,00,000):
- Enhanced beneficiary verification recommended
- Check beneficiary first-time flag — new beneficiary triggers cooling period (many banks enforce 24-hour wait)
- Behavioral score threshold: ≥ 0.85
- Trusted partner auto-approve: score ≥ 0.88
- Suitable for: business payments, loan disbursements, property advance

HIGH RISK (₹5,00,001 – ₹25,00,000):
- HITL review recommended for first-time beneficiaries
- Mandatory dual-authorization for corporate accounts
- Verify source of funds documentation
- Behavioral score threshold: ≥ 0.92
- Consider RTGS as preferred channel for higher assurance
- Trusted partner auto-approve: score ≥ 0.95

VERY HIGH RISK (above ₹25,00,000):
- Recommend RTGS instead (real-time gross settlement)
- If NEFT chosen: mandatory HITL review + source-of-funds check
- AML (Anti-Money Laundering) screening required above ₹10,00,000
- CTR (Cash Transaction Report) filing if linked to cash deposit
- STR (Suspicious Transaction Report) if unusual pattern detected

Trusted Partner Framework for NEFT:
- Tier 1 (Established — 20+ successful NEFT transfers, 6+ months): auto-approve up to ₹5L
- Tier 2 (Regular — 5–19 transfers, 3+ months): auto-approve up to ₹2L
- Tier 3 (New — <5 transfers): HITL review above ₹1L, cooling period for new beneficiary
- Corporate partners: enhanced limits per bilateral agreement

When to Recommend NEFT:
- Non-urgent transfers of any amount
- Batch salary/vendor disbursements
- When RTGS minimum (₹2L) is too high for the transaction
- When UPI amount limit (₹1L standard) is too low
- Recurring scheduled payments where exact timing is flexible
- Preferred over cash/cheque for audit trail and instant confirmation

Fraud Patterns Specific to NEFT:
- Beneficiary impersonation: wrong IFSC/account combination sends to unintended recipient
- Email compromise (BEC): fraudster changes beneficiary details in payment instructions
- Mule accounts: money laundering via rapid NEFT transfers through multiple accounts
- After-hours bulk fraud: automated fraud during off-peak monitoring hours
Mitigation: beneficiary name validation (NPCI Positive Pay analog), cooling periods for new beneficiaries, velocity monitoring
