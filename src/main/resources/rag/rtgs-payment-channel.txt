RTGS (Real Time Gross Settlement) — Indian Payment Channel Risk & Routing Guide

Overview:
RTGS is India's large-value, real-time fund transfer system operated by the Reserve Bank of India. Unlike NEFT (batch-based), RTGS settles transactions individually and instantly on a gross basis. It is the preferred channel for high-value urgent payments, inter-bank settlement, and critical business transactions.

Transaction Limits:
- Minimum transfer: ₹2,00,000 (mandatory floor set by RBI)
- Maximum transfer: No upper limit
- Bank-specific daily limits may apply via net banking (typically ₹25,00,000 to ₹100,00,000 for retail, higher for corporates)
- Corporate/institutional: limits set per agreement with bank

Settlement: Real-time gross settlement (individual transaction, not batched)
- Transfer completes within 30 minutes (typically 2–10 minutes)
- Operating hours: 24x7 since December 14, 2020 (earlier 7 AM – 6 PM on weekdays)
- Finality: irrevocable once settled — cannot be reversed by sender's bank

Charges (RBI mandated — effective July 2019):
- Savings account online: ZERO charges (RBI mandated, no fee for online RTGS)
- Offline / branch-initiated: ₹25 (₹2L–₹5L), ₹50 (above ₹5L) + GST
- Beneficiary bank: No charges on incoming RTGS
- Corporate pricing: as per bank agreement, generally ₹20–₹50 per transaction

Required Details:
- Beneficiary name, bank account number, IFSC code
- Remitter identification (KYC-compliant bank account)
- Purpose code for cross-border related settlements

Risk Profile by Transaction Amount:
HIGH VALUE – STANDARD (₹2,00,000 – ₹10,00,000):
- Minimum RTGS tier, standard for business payments
- Beneficiary verification mandatory (name match validation)
- Behavioral score threshold: ≥ 0.88
- Trusted partner auto-approve: score ≥ 0.90
- Suitable for: property advances, vehicle purchases, business vendor payments

HIGH VALUE – ELEVATED (₹10,00,001 – ₹50,00,000):
- Enhanced due diligence recommended
- HITL review for first-time beneficiary or new relationship
- Cross-reference with existing transaction patterns
- AML screening mandatory (RBI PMLA guidelines)
- Dual-authorization for corporate accounts
- Behavioral score threshold: ≥ 0.92
- Trusted partner auto-approve: score ≥ 0.95

VERY HIGH VALUE (₹50,00,001 – ₹2,00,00,000):
- Mandatory HITL review regardless of trust score
- Source-of-funds documentation required
- AML/CFT (Combating Financing of Terrorism) screening
- STR (Suspicious Transaction Report) evaluation
- Senior operator approval required
- Purpose of payment declaration mandatory

ULTRA HIGH VALUE (above ₹2,00,00,000):
- Multi-level approval chain: operator → senior → compliance officer
- Real-time RBI reporting for transactions above ₹10 crore
- Pre-transaction verification call to remitter (for individual accounts)
- Must comply with FEMA regulations if cross-border element exists
- Full audit trail with timestamp, IP, device, and operator ID

Trusted Partner Framework for RTGS:
- Tier 1 (Established — 10+ successful RTGS, registered entity, 1+ year): auto-approve up to ₹10L
- Tier 2 (Regular — 3–9 RTGS transfers, 6+ months): auto-approve up to ₹5L
- Tier 3 (New — <3 RTGS or <6 months): mandatory HITL review for all RTGS transactions
- Institutional partners: custom thresholds per bilateral agreement with compliance sign-off

When to Recommend RTGS:
- Any urgent payment of ₹2,00,000 or above
- Real-time settlement certainty required (property, securities, legal settlements)
- When NEFT batch delay is unacceptable
- Large business-to-business payments
- Merger/acquisition-related fund transfers
- When irrevocability and finality are required for legal or contractual reasons

Fraud Patterns Specific to RTGS:
- Business Email Compromise (BEC): sophisticated fraud targeting high-value corporate payments
- CEO fraud: impersonation of senior executive to authorize urgent RTGS transfer
- Invoice manipulation: altered bank details on legitimate invoices
- Settlement timing exploitation: fraudster exploits 24x7 availability during off-hours with reduced monitoring
- Irrevocability abuse: social engineering to convince victim to send RTGS (cannot be reversed)
Mitigation: mandatory callback verification for first-time high-value beneficiary, dual-authorization, 4-eyes principle for amounts above ₹50L, real-time AML screening
