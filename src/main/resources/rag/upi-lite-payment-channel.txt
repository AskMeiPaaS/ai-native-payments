UPI Lite — Indian Payment Channel Risk & Routing Guide

⚠️ HARD LIMIT: UPI Lite is ONLY for amounts ≤ ₹500. Any amount above ₹500 MUST NOT use UPI Lite — use UPI, NEFT, or RTGS instead. This rule has no exceptions.

Overview:
UPI Lite is a lightweight, on-device wallet feature built on top of the Unified Payments Interface (UPI) ecosystem, introduced by NPCI and RBI in September 2022. It is optimised for high-volume, low-value (micro) payments where speed and offline-readiness are paramount. Funds are pre-loaded from the user's linked bank account into an on-device UPI Lite wallet; debit transactions happen locally without a bank server round-trip, making payments significantly faster and more resilient to network fluctuations. No UPI PIN is required for UPI Lite transactions, reducing friction to near-zero.

Key Differentiators from Standard UPI:
- No UPI PIN required for every debit — wallet pre-authorised at top-up time
- Transactions processed on-device; no bank auth server call per payment
- Faster settlement: sub-second merchant confirmation in most scenarios
- Designed for contactless / NFC tap-to-pay at offline merchants
- Cannot be used for P2P collect requests or recurring mandates
- Exclusively for real-time, push-only (payer-initiated) payments

Regulatory Basis:
- RBI circular on Payments Infrastructure Development Fund, Sep 2022
- NPCI UPI Lite product circular NPCI/UPI/OC No. 148/2022-23
- RBI Master Direction on Prepaid Payment Instruments (PPIs) — UPI Lite treated as a limited-purpose PPI

Transaction Limits (RBI-mandated, as of 2024–2025):
- Maximum per transaction: ₹500
- Maximum wallet balance: ₹2,000
- No minimum transaction amount
- No limit on number of transactions per day from the wallet
- Top-up (load) limit: ₹2,000 per load, minimum ₹100 per load
- Wallet auto-reload: supported via UPI AutoPay mandate (below ₹15,000/month)

Settlement:
- Debit: Instant, on-device
- Merchant credit: Real-time via NPCI switch (treated same as UPI P2M settlement)
- Top-up debit from bank: Instant UPI debit to pre-load wallet

Charges:
- Zero MDR for merchants (government-mandated, same as standard UPI)
- No transaction fee for end users
- No charges for wallet top-up

Availability:
- 24x7x365
- Offline-capable: transactions succeed even during intermittent internet outages (debit queued on-device, settled when connectivity resumes)
- Supported apps: Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, BHIM UPI, and major bank UPI apps

Use Cases (Best-Fit Scenarios):
- payments under ₹500
- Auto-rickshaw / metro / bus tap-to-pay fares
- Vending machine payments
- Small grocery / kirana store purchases
- Coffee shop / QSR (Quick Service Restaurant) payments
- Micro-loan repayments under ₹500
- In-app micro-purchases (gaming, streaming top-ups)

When to Auto-Select UPI Lite (System Rule):
If a transfer or receive request does NOT specify a payment channel AND the amount is ≤ ₹500, UPI Lite MUST be selected automatically. No user confirmation or PIN prompt should be shown. This is the primary routing rule for micro-payments in the PaSS platform.
CRITICAL: If the amount is ABOVE ₹500, do NOT select UPI Lite under any circumstances. Use UPI (up to ₹1,00,000), NEFT (up to ₹2,00,000), or RTGS (₹2,00,000 and above) instead.

Risk Profile:
LOW RISK (₹0 – ₹500 — entire UPI Lite range):
- All UPI Lite transactions are inherently low-value and low-risk
- No PIN friction — wallet was pre-authorised at top-up
- Minimal fraud monitoring overhead required
- Auto-approve for all users without HITL review
- Velocity cap: monitor if >50 transactions/hour from single wallet (may indicate compromised device)

Fraud Patterns Specific to UPI Lite:
- Device theft: stolen device with unlocked screen can spend wallet balance up to ₹2,000 without PIN — risk is limited to wallet ceiling (₹2,000 maximum exposure)
- Malicious app draining wallet: rogue merchant app initiates repeated ₹500 collections
- Wallet reload abuse: rapid top-ups followed by immediate spend to launder small amounts
- QR code substitution at offline merchants: merchant QR replaced by fraudster QR in low-supervision settings
Mitigation: device binding, wallet balance ceiling (₹2,000 hard limit), per-session velocity monitoring, merchant QR integrity checks

Trusted Partner Framework for UPI Lite:
- No tiered trust framework required — all transactions auto-approved (amount ceiling provides natural risk control)
- New users: auto-approve from day one (no cooling period for amounts ≤ ₹500)
- Trusted partner score threshold: N/A (below HITL trigger floor)

HITL Escalation Policy:
- Never escalate UPI Lite transactions to HITL — amount ceiling eliminates need
- Exception: if fraud score > 0.95 from behavioural model (extremely rare for ₹500 transactions)

Channel Eligibility Summary:
- ELIGIBLE: Any payment ≤ ₹500 where payer initiates the transfer
- NOT ELIGIBLE: Amounts > ₹500 (use UPI, NEFT, RTGS, or Cheque)
- NOT ELIGIBLE: P2P collect requests (payer must push; payee cannot pull via UPI Lite)
- NOT ELIGIBLE: Recurring mandates / UPI AutoPay debits
- NOT ELIGIBLE: International transactions

Relationship to Standard UPI:
UPI Lite is a sub-product of UPI, not a separate payment rail. The same VPA (Virtual Payment Address) and UPI app is used. The key distinction is that UPI Lite funds are held in an on-device wallet pre-loaded from the bank account, whereas standard UPI debits the bank account directly for each transaction. Both settle through the NPCI switch to the merchant's bank account in real time.

Comparison with Other Channels for Sub-₹500 Amounts:
- vs. UPI (standard): UPI Lite is faster (no PIN, no bank round-trip); prefer UPI Lite for ≤₹500
- vs. NEFT: UPI Lite is real-time; NEFT is batch (30 min delay); UPI Lite preferred
- vs. RTGS: RTGS minimum is ₹2,00,000 — not applicable for sub-₹500
- vs. Cheque: Cheque is offline, slow, paper-based — UPI Lite preferred for all digital scenarios
- vs. Net Banking: UPI Lite has no login friction; Net Banking requires browser session — UPI Lite preferred
